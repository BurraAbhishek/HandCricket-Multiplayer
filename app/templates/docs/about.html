About this site

This site is a free, adless, open-source hand cricket server, powered by volunteers.

There are several servers for board games, but making one for a team game is not easy. In addition to that, there are several online sports games which you can play, but most of them serve advertisements, or contain hidden trackers and paywalls.

Cricket is an outdoor sport. During the COVID-19 pandemic, several safety measures were implemented by governments. People were not allowed to play in groups to protect themselves from the deadly SARS-CoV-2 virus. Thus, many resorted to playing online.

Online cricket is not as effective as outdoor cricket. Thus, to make things simpler, this site hosts a much simpler mode of playing cricket. Despite being hand cricket, the Laws of Cricket which can be implemented in online settings are fully supported.

The initial idea was to fork the source code of Lichess, the most popular free and open-source online chess server. However, we soon came to realize that major rewriting was needed, since analysis, learning and puzzles were not a part of this hand cricket website.

The logic of the game is already available on GitHub. This site will use the same logic, since that is also open-source. For this reason, this site is written using Django instead of the Play Framework as was earlier planned.

Nevertheless, this site will be forever free and open-source without any advertisements or trackers. Major pieces of layout and text were copied from Lichess, although the UI differs in color and dimensions. So a big word of thanks to Thibault Duplessis for his brilliant piece of work lichess.org, the other lichess developers and the open-source community in general.

This site is still in its early stages of development; there are going to be bugs and missing features, but we are trying to improve the site as much as we can. The source code is available on GitHub.
